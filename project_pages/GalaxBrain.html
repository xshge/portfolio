    This game is a solo 3 week project for my Core Game Design class, and we were working off of an open theme: Spark. I started off listing synonyms and connection to the word. Then, I picked out the ones that stood out to me, and then I wrote out gameplay ideas that might be correlating to those connections. However, those exercises still created a framework that is too broad for me to fully conceptualize a game. In order to narrow further down, I decided to look towards games that I believe can give me inspiration, in which I further discussed this process in my full design documentation for this project.

    Since this game is set out to be a puzzle game, with its electrical wiring concept, I went through an extensive iterative process in order to create these levels. The stages of this process were also presented through the colors of sticky notes that I used for designing these levels. The yellow sticky notes were the initial puzzles after I had created the tools and components for the game. These are the blueprints for my later levels, after the first round of feedback for the complete game play. The green stick notes are the modified versions of some of the yellow levels after the initial feedback. The pink sticky notes are a combination of modified levels and new levels after the second round of feedback. The pink ones are where I really go into more exploration with the limits of my puzzles and have gained more insights into the actual appeals of them from the last round of feedback through these levels.
