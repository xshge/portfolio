Rapid Game Prototypes for 13 weeks, Part 3

12-10-2024

≡

This blog is part 3 of me going through all the protoypes that I created for my Core Game Design Class. Each week we would have a different theme for our protoypes, and there are some of my thoughts on the theme and the end result.

Last Part Link to all the games that is mentioned in this blog

Wk8,9,10: Customizer, Sequel & Just Add World:

These three weeks will be combined as they were all based on the same idea that I decided to extrapolate. There were two renditions of this idea; one was an active rag doll with customizable limbs, and the latter one, which was also the final decision was an active ragdoll that climbs. I had always wanted to experiment with Unity’s physics engine, so I thought an active ragdoll would be a fitting thing to create for this learning process.

For week eight, there was definitely a lot of time that was put in for creating a usable ragdoll, and after creating it, I also came to the realization that making parts customizable would most likely mess up the balance of the doll. Due to the complexity of the task, I scratched the idea and I just made a ragdoll that players can put accessories on while playing in the playground environment that I set up. I was proud of what I was able to achieve, and I also learned about Scriptable Objects in Unity. Also, people enjoyed the wacky nature of the ragdoll and the simple task of just playing in the environment. However, I was not done with this ragdoll just yet.

week 8 ragdoll

Week nine and ten go hand in hand, as one is just a slightly better and more experimental version of the other. Week nine is just about us going back and adding to one of our preexisting prototypes. I took the opportunity to go back and further improve my ragdoll prototype by adding a purposefully janky climbing ability to it, which created a gameplay that is akin to the feel of games like Getting Over It. For this week, I didn’t make many head waves with the level design of the game, but I did find a way to make the tutorials more engaging by emulating an '80s at-home workout instruction video. The instruction still kind of holds players’ hands, but at least it was more engaging than the pet life version.

Week ten is an extension of week nine, as I fixed some bugs and added a procedurally generated climbing wall for this ragdoll. Originally, I intended the wall to be a case study of level design in A Difficult Game about Climbing and Getting Over It, however, with the requirement of procedural generation, this task has been proven to be a bit more difficult and complex for one week. There were still elements of intentions in this procedurally generated version of the wall, but this version still lacked the ambiguous path-finding elements that both games share. Also, just in general, I wished there was more control over the generation, but that will probably require more rules to be set. Last, at least, people still enjoyed it because of the challenging nature that came from controlling a ragdoll.

Wk11 Who would win??:

This is the last week before the final, and we were tasked to create an auto battler. Honestly, if my event system hadn’t bug out on the last day, I would have created more levels for this game. I still enjoyed designing a combat system, and I wanted to design a system where the player only has defensive measures to ensure the safety of their villagers. In the future, I might come back to further develop this prototype and add the features that I ran out of time for.

Wk12 Spark:

This week is our final week and it is an open theme. The duration of this project extends to the end of our semester, so we had plenty of time to create a complete game. The idea is to create a multi-level puzzle game that centers around the theme of growing brain cells by making neuron connections in the brain. I am pretty satisfied with the outcome and there is a page dedicated to talking about the making of this game and the full design document is on that page as well.
